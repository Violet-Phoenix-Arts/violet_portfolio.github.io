Poetry!

It's a bit pretentious and nerdy, but I enjoy writing tankas/haikus just for fun.
Most of these were never designed to be seen by anyone (or at least not by anyone that knows me), but they are a creative work to supplement my other stuff
I tend to write these in the moment when I find anything for inspiration, and looking back through them, a lot of them sound way more morbid than they are intended to be.

"what's a tanka?" I don't blame you for being unfamiliar with the term, as they are very often overshadowed by their cousin the haiku.

A tanka is a 5 line non rhyming poem signified by a 5-7-5-7-7 syllable scheme, and often with a "scene shift"?(can't find the word, oopsie) on either the 2nd or 3rd lines.


This first one is supposed to show a contrast between the perpetuity of the universe with the fragility of our experiences and life.

Beautiful stars shine
Interwoven nebulas
Eyes turn down to earth
Litter piles up on our shrines
Do the stars remain the same?


My second tanka is supposed to be about how history repeats. We can see the future by comparing what has happened with what is happening.

The stars see our past
The lakes reflect our present
What shows our future?
We read the flow of sea and sky
And find what tomorrow brings


Now my third poem in very similar in spirit to the first, but more grounded one earth. Ignoring the stars, our lives are simply blips even compared to our own planet and creations

Metal lines the streets
Greenery grows in concrete
Yet the sun still shines
Birds still sing and grass still grows
Nature will outlast us all


This poem is about the video game Hollow Knight. It's supposed be a critique of the pale kings desire for "an eternal kindom" via stagnation. The only way to make something truly eternal, is to allow it to change.

Lifeblood stains the ground
Infection and void reclaim
And the world decays
From the rot rises new life
A true eternal kingdom


Finally, this poem is about an end to struggle. It's way more morbid than I intended it to be, the ending is not supposed to be death specifically, but peace.

Golden sun burns me
Soft grass beneath drags me down
I look to the sky
And close my eyes at last
What a beautiful ending
